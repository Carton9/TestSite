Matter

  • Describing Matter:
    • Mass, Volume, Extensive Property, Intensive Property, Substance.
    • States of Matter:
      • Solid: a form of matter that has a definite shape and volume
      • Gas: a form of matter that takes both the shape and volume of its container
      • Liquid: a form of matter that has an indefinite shape, flows, and yet has a fixed volume
    • Physical Change:
      • A change that materials property changes, but the composition is not changing.
      • Can be classified as reversible and irreversible.

Mixtures

  • Classifying Mixtures:
    • In a heterogeneous mixture, the components are not evenly distributed.
    • In a homogeneous mixture, or solution, the components are evenly distributed.
    • A heterogeneous mixture consists of at least two phase, but a homogeneous mixture contains exactly one phase.
  • Separating Mixtures:
    • A solid and a liquid can be separated by filtration because of the difference in the physical properties of the two components.
    • A liquid solution might be separated by distillation, during which the component with the lowest boiling point is boiled off, and the resulting vapors are then condensed.
  • Mixtures—physical blend 2+ component (air, coffee)
    • Coffee, vinegar is homogeneous mixtures, steel, iron, nickel, chromium
    • Oil+ water or oil + vinegar is heterogeneous mixtures
    • Sun is heterogeneous mixture
  • Air is homogenous mixture
  • Filtration-separate a solid from a liquid in a heterogeneous mixture (like caffebene from brewed coffee)
  • Distillation (separate tap water with other components, boiling point)
  • Element:
    • the simplest form of matter that has a unique set of properties. Ex: Oxygen Hydrogen.
  • Compound:
    • a substance that contains two or more elements chemically combined in a fixed proportion .
  • What is the different?
    • compounds can be broken down into simpler substances, but elements cannot.
  • Compounds only can be broken down by chemical changes. Ex: heating

Change

  • Chemical Changes: During a chemical change, the composition of matter change.
    • A chemical change must occur for a chemical property to be observed.
    • The substances at the beginning of reaction = reactants.
    • The substances produced by the reaction = products.
    • Reactants and products differ in composition.
  • Conservation of Mass: The law of conservation of mass says that mass is neither created nor destroyed during a normal chemical reaction or physical change.
    • During chemical reactions, the total mass of reactants equal the total mass if the products.
    • Mass is also conserved during physical changes, such as a change of state.
