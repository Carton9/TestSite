Thermochemistry

  • Thermochemistry
    • The study of changes in heat energy that accompany chemical reactions and physical changes
  • Heat
    • Represented by q, the energy flow that transfers from one object to another because of the temperature difference
  • Enthalpy
    • Represented by H, the heat content of a system at constant pressure
  • Temperature
    • A measure of the average kinetic energy of the particles in a sample

Energy

  • The ability to do work
  • Conservation of Energy
    • Energy cannot be created or destroyed
    • Kinetic Energy- Energy of motion
    • Potential Energy- Stored energy
  • Collision Theory
    • The assumption that for reactions to occur, collisions between a substance’s particles must occur
  • Activation Energy
    • The minimum energy required to start the reaction
    • Denoted by the symbol: Ea
  • Endothermic Reaction
    • A reaction in which heat is absorbed from the surroundings
  • Exothermic Reaction
    • A reaction in which heat is released into the surroundings
  • Catalyst
    • A catalyst is a substance that changes the rate of a chemical reaction without itself being consumed

Thermochemical Equation

  • An equation that includes the quantity of heat released or absorbed during the reaction

Equilibrium

  • Reversible Reactions
    • A chemical reaction in which the products can react to re-form the reactants
  • Chemical Equilibrium
    • When the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction and the concentration of products and reactants remains unchanged

Approaching Equilibrium

  • Reactant concentrations start high and decrease
  • forward rate(which depends on collisions of the reactants)decreases.
  • Product concentrations start at zero and increase
  • reverse rate(which depends on collisions of the products)increases
  • Equilibrium
    • forward rate is equal to the reverse rate
    • the concentrations are constant.
